About me

Within this section, I'll provide a cheesy explaination of who I am and my interests, so here goes...

Hello World! My name is Yasser, a PhD student at the University of Warwick. Throughout my life, I have been enthralled by technology and its applications. As such, I have completed a BEng in Systems Engineering and have begun my journey in my PhD.

My work within my PhD involves exploring mosquito flight behaviour with the aim of improving disease prevention mechanisms. Currently, around 700 million people are affected globally by mosquito related diseases and so improvements in disease prevention mechanisms is a crucial area that needs to be explored. My research specifically deals with mosquito trajectory data where I attempt to explain and explore the various behvaioural traits that different mosquito species possess.

Outside of my work, I continue to enjoy developing various software solutions for my personal passions - namely sneaker collecting! On the otherhand, I am a keen sportsman with long track record of competing in athletics (excuse the pun!) - in particular, I am a 400m runner!
