Curvature Scale Space

Curvature scale space (CSS) is a shape descriptor for planar curves. Introduced by Mokhtarian and Mackworth, its main uses lies in object recognition, where matching algorithms can be applied to identify shapes within images.

23 Dec 2021 · 2 min. read
Share this

Shape descriptors are features that describe shapes through various metrics. Within this article, I will describe curvature scale space as a useful feature for applications such as object recognition and trajectory analysis. It was introduced by Mokhtarian and Mackworth as a representation of a curve that encompasses the following properties:

  • Rotation invariant
  • Invariant under uniform scaling
  • Translation invariant
Essentially, this method works by finding points of inflection on the curve at varying levels smoothing. We can then represent the points of inflection using a CSS image. Let's look at it a bit closer.

Let a curve be defined expressed as two functions \(x(t)\) and \(y(t)\): $$C = \{ x(t), y(t) \}$$ The curvature at a point \(P\) on the curve is given by: $$ \kappa = \frac{\dot x \ddot y - \ddot x \dot y}{(\dot x^2 - \dot y^2)^\frac{3}{2}} $$ To compute the curvature at varying levels of detail (i.e. different smoothing levels), we convolve the functions \(x(t)\) and \(y(t)\) with a one-dimensional Gaussian kernel \(g(t, \sigma)\) of width \(\sigma\). $$ g(t, \sigma) = \frac{1}{\sigma \sqrt{2\pi}} e^{-t^2 / 2\sigma^2} $$ As we increase the value of \(\sigma\), we find points of inflection and mark them on a scale space image with axis \(t\) and \(\sigma\). The value of \(\sigma\) is increased until no inflection points are found. Visually, when we increase \(\sigma\), we are smoothing the curve.
